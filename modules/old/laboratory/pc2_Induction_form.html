Level 3 PC2 Laboratory Induction

For access to the Woolcock Level 3 PC2 Laboratories you need to complete the following:

1. Demonstrate protection to diseases as outlined in Policy 6.6.5. Occupational Screening and Vaccinations. If you are to be involved in lung dissections you will also require tuberculosis monitoring.

2. Enrol in 2 x half day courses on "Biosafety" and "Working with Chemicals" respectively. These are run by Sydney University and are free. Instructions on how to enrol will be provided to you in your Lab Induction Manual. An email confirming enrolment on each of these courses should be forwarded to the Laboratory Manager. Copies of certificates for completion of these courses must be sent to the Laboratory Manager within 6 months of starting the Induction process. Laboratory Manager: Lyn Moir (lyn.moir@sydney.edu.au)

3. Undergo an on-site induction with the Laboratory Manager. The Induction will involve walking through the laboratory facilities and outlining regulatory compliance items as well as other specific work practices as documented in the "Conditions of Access (Lab Rules). It will not involve instruction on specific laboratory protocols and procedures which remain the responsibility of your supervisor.

4. Complete an online Induction Quiz.

All Woolcock staff, students, affiliates and visitors are expected to comply with all Woolcock policies and procedures.

Safety Data Sheets (SDS), previously known as a Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS) are standard reference documents for chemical information.  They provide people and the emergency service personnel essential information about the following:

Basic physical and chemical properties of the chemical.

Correct safety procedures when storing, handling, transporting and disposing of the product.

Health hazards and impacts on the environment. 

What to do in accidents and emergencies.

In Australia they must be reviewed and if necessary revised at least every five years.

ChemAlert is a comprehensive database of SDSs available on The University of Sydney network
